01 — Client

Meet G-Park
Thailand

G-Park develops automated parking systems for space-constrained urban environments. Based in Bangkok, their portfolio spans seven distinct technologies — from Tower systems that stack cars vertically to AGV systems using autonomous guided vehicles for transport.

When they approached Craf-T, they had a fragmented marketing approach. Seven separate videos. Seven separate messages. No unified entry point for potential clients.

They needed one video that could tell the whole story.

01
Development
~5 hours
Working without a detailed creative brief, Craf-T began with deep comprehension. The team studied reference materials — photos and videos of real parking installations — to understand not just how each system worked, but why it mattered. A reverse storyboard approach captured the hero frame from each scene.
VISUAL NARRATIVE — 7 Sequential Sketches

Reference AnalysisStoryboardingMood Board
02
Pre-Production
~5 hours
Rough animatics established timing for all seven system segments within the 56-second constraint. Camera paths and transitions were blocked out to ensure smooth visual flow between segments. Every system earned its moment without overstaying its welcome.
5 / 100 hours
BlenderAnimaticsCamera Blocking
03
Production
~75 hours
All seven parking systems were modeled from scratch in Blender using only photos and videos as reference. No CAD files. No technical drawings. Soft, diffused studio lighting highlighted mechanical details without harsh shadows. Animation was simulation-grade — natural, realistic motion representing real-world operation. Rendered in 4K at 30fps across approximately 1,680 frames.
PRODUCTION — Blender Viewport
75 / 100 hours

04
Post-Production
~15 hours
DaVinci Resolve handled professional color grading for visual consistency across all seven segments. Adobe Premiere added system identification text overlays and refined timing. Final export optimized for multiple delivery formats including broadcast and large-format displays.
